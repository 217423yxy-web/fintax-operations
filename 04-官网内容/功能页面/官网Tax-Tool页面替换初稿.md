Per AICPA's Digital Asset Audit Practice Guide, there are three ways to obtain blockchain data. Running your own node is costly; free block explorers lack verifiable reliability. The recommended approach: engage a SOC-certified third-party provider that meets AU-C 402 standards.

| Capability | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **SOC 1 & SOC 2 Type II** | Control effectiveness independently tested over the full reporting period |
| **Auditor-ready data** | Meets GAAS evidence requirements — no additional alternative procedures needed |
| **Three core control objectives** | Data integrity, accuracy, and timeliness — all within SOC audit scope |

**Multi-Node Cross-Verification**

- Multiple independent nodes per chain, cross-validating results to eliminate single-point errors
- Every record includes block height and transaction hash — traceable to the original blockchain record
- Full evidence chain (data source, retrieval time, node info) ready for audit workpapers

**Reports That Fit Directly Into Workpapers**

CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework) is the digital-asset equivalent of CRS, requiring exchanges to report transaction-level data to tax authorities across 70+ jurisdictions. The global compliance deadline is 2027, with system readiness expected by end of 2026.

**What SOC certifications does Audit Tool hold?**
FinTax Audit Tool holds both SOC 1 and SOC 2 Type II certifications, independently audited by a CPA firm. This means auditors can directly rely on our data without performing additional alternative procedures.

**How many blockchains does Audit Tool support?**
Audit Tool currently supports 15+ public chains including Bitcoin, Ethereum, BSC, Polygon, Arbitrum, Solana, and Avalanche, with coverage for native tokens, ERC-20/BEP-20 tokens, NFTs, and DeFi staking positions.
